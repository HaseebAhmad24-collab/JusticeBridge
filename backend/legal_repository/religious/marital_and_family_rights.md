# Marital and Family Rights in Islam and Pakistan - Legal Guide

This document covers the rights and obligations of spouses and family members under Sharia and the Muslim Family Laws Ordinance (MFLO) 1961.

## 1. Nikah (Marriage)
*   **Contract:** Marriage in Islam is a civil contract (Nikah) based on mutual consent (Ijab-o-Qabul).
*   **Essentials:**
    - Proposers (Groom & Bride).
    - Witnesses (Two adult males or one male and two females).
    - Haq Mehr (Dower): A mandatory gift from the groom to the bride.
*   **Registration:** Under the MFLO, every marriage must be registered with the Nikah Registrar in the Nikah Nama.

## 2. Haq Mehr (Dower)
*   **Prompt (Mu'ajjal):** Payable immediately at the time of Nikah.
*   **Deferred (Mu'wajjal):** Payable on death or dissolution of marriage.
*   The wife has an absolute right to her dower, and it cannot be waived under duress.

## 3. Dissolution of Marriage (Talaq & Khula)
### Talaq (Divorce by Husband)
*   The husband has the unilateral right to divorce. 
*   **Notice:** Under Section 7 of the MFLO, the husband must send a written notice to the Chairman of the Union Council after pronouncing Talaq. The divorce becomes effective 90 days after the notice, allowing for reconciliation.

### Khula (Divorce by Wife through Court)
*   The wife can seek Khula from a Family Court if she can no longer live with her husband "within the limits of Allah."
*   Usually requires the wife to return her dower or waive it.

### Talaq-e-Tafweez (Delegated Divorce)
*   The husband can delegate his right of divorce to the wife in the Nikah Nama (Clause 18).

## 4. Maintenance (Nafaqa)
*   The husband is legally bound to provide food, clothing, and shelter to his wife throughout the marriage and during the Iddat period.
*   Failure to provide maintenance can be a ground for dissolution of marriage.

## 5. Child Custody (Hazanat)
*   **General Rule:** The mother has the right of Hazanat (custody) for young children:
    - Sons: Up to 7 years.
    - Daughters: Up to puberty.
*   **Welfare of the Child:** The court's primary consideration is always the "Welfare of the Minor." Custody can be shifted if the welfare is at risk.
*   **Father's Role:** The father remains the natural guardian (Wali) and is responsible for all financial support even if the child is in the mother's custody.
