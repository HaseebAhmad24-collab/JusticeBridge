# Islamic Law of Inheritance (Meeras) - Comprehensive Guide

Inheritance in Islam is a divine decree (Faraid) defined primarily in the Quran (Surah An-Nisa).

## 1. General Principles
*   **Estate:** Inheritance applies only after payment of funeral expenses, debts, and any valid bequest (up to 1/3 of the remaining estate).
*   **Heirs:** Heirs are categorized into:
    1.  **Sharers (Zul-Faraid):** Those whose shares are fixed by the Quran.
    2.  **Residuaries (Asaba):** Those who take the remainder after sharers are satisfied.
    3.  **Distant Kindred (Zul-Arham):** Other relatives who inherit if no sharers or residuaries exist.

## 2. Fixed Shares (Primary Sharers)
### Husband's Share
*   **1/2** if there are no children or grandchildren.
*   **1/4** if there are children or grandchildren.

### Wife's Share
*   **1/4** if there are no children or grandchildren.
*   **1/8** if there are children or grandchildren (Multiple wives share this 1/8 equally).

### Father's Share
*   **1/6** as a fixed share if there are children.
*   Inherits as a residuary if there are no male children.

### Mother's Share
*   **1/6** if there are children or two or more siblings.
*   **1/3** if there are no children and no more than one sibling.

## 3. Shares of Children
*   **Son's Share:** Sons are always residuaries and never fixed sharers.
*   **Daughter's Share:** 
    *   **1/2** if she is an only child.
    *   **2/3** (shared) if there are two or more daughters and no son.
*   **Rule of Two-to-One:** If there are both sons and daughters, the son receives twice the share of the daughter.

## 4. Impediments to Inheritance (Mahjubiyat)
*   **Homicide:** A person who kills the deceased cannot inherit from them.
*   **Different Religion:** In classical Sharia, a non-Muslim does not inherit from a Muslim relative (though modern Pakistani laws like the Succession Act may have specific interpretations for non-Muslim citizens).

## 5. Grandchildren (Mabroot)
Under the **Muslim Family Laws Ordinance 1961 (Pakistan)**, the children of a predeceased son or daughter inherit the share their parent would have received if alive. (This is a specific statutory law in Pakistan that differs from classical Fiqh).
